Context¶

The dataset stores detailed information from the top 1000 Youtube channels based on the quantity of subscribers. Along with the name, ranking and subscribers, there are other columns related to the quantity of views, category, country and minimum and maximum level of earnings, among other useful features for analysis. As you can see, we can approach this dataset in a lot of different ways!

For this occasion, we will analyze in a general way the distribution of subscribers and views on these features and try to find relations in the data. We would also want to find answers on what to do to engage with people and therefore, get more views and subscribers to our channel.

  • We could create a new feature called loyalty, which consists in the mean proportion of subscribers that watch the videos when uploaded. We can calculate this column by dividing the total views by the quantity of uploaded videos (which is the mean views per video uploaded), and then dividing this value by the number of subscribers. This is an approximation, because it does not consider multiple views by one user on the same video and neither the distribution of the views (non subscriber/subscriber). Nonetheless, it is useful to measure the reach that the videos have.

countries_population = {}
countries_languages = {}

for ab in df['Abbreviation'].unique():
    if ab is np.nan:
        continue
        
    population = pypopulation.get_population(ab)
    country_identifier = 'und_'+ab
    country_language = Locale.parse(country_identifier).language
    
    countries_languages[ab] = country_language
    if population is None:
        print('None')
    else:
        countries_population[ab] = population
    
df['country population'] = df['Abbreviation'].replace(countries_population)
df['country language'] = df['Abbreviation'].replace(countries_languages)

Earnings¶

  • First of all we calculate a column of average monthly earnings (simply called mean earnings), to have an approximate value and not an interval. This will allow us to contrast the monthly earnings with other variables. In addition, to know more recently how much money each view generates, we divide these earnings by the views of the last 30 days.

  • In order to eliminate from the analysis the official Youtube channels and other channels that do not necessarily generate earnings, a filter is made (the official Youtube channels such as Music or Gaming do not have earnings or views). In this way, it can be seen that the earnings per view do not differ by category and remain practically identical among them, with an average of 2.1 USD per 1000 views.

Conclusions¶

  • Although the largest number of subscribers is concentrated in a couple of countries, it is not necessary to be originally from a particular country to have a greater impact of views/subscribers. What is important to consider is the language of the content generated. Almost half of the total views come from channels that produce content in English (or very likely, at least), and this is without counting the channels that upload content in English but are not from a country where English is an official language. Globally, it is the language with the most speakers (over 1.4 billion), so it is an important factor for any channel to reach more people.

  • Although subscribers are a factor directly related to the number of views, not all of them will watch every video uploaded from a channel but a relatively variable percentage that probably depends on other variables not demonstrated in this analysis (frequency of uploads, monotony of content, youtube drama, etc). However, in order to grow and keep up with these Youtube giants it is also important to have a reach outside of this subscriber base, especially of new viewers interested in the content who understand the language, thus increasing both views and subscribers to the channel.

  • Along the same lines as the previous point, there is strong evidence of international users in the most influential channels. Most likely, the content developed by these channels cuts across different categories or is highly appealing to a certain group of the world population (e.g. children's audience in the case of several channels in the dataset).

  • A higher number of uploads does not necessarily mean more views, in fact it depends to a greater extent on the category. In this sense, the most influential channels belong to the Entertainment, Music, People & Blogs and Gaming categories.

  • Views and average earnings have a strong linear relationship. The higher the views, the higher the earnings.

  • Earnings per view do not vary by category, and on average are $2.1 per 1000 views, a little less than the $4 to $20 that Youtube pays for the same amount of views (based on approximations). What does affect earnings are the categories, and the ones that generate the most earnings are obviously also the ones that collect the most views (Entertainment, Music and People & Blogs).
